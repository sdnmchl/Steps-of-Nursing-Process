4.Implementation:"Putting the plan into action!"


The implementation phase of the nursing process is when the nurse puts the treatment plan into effect. It involves action or doing and the actual carrying out of specific nursing interventions, focusing on achievable outcomes outlined in the plan of care. It typically begins with the medical staff conducting any needed medical interventions.

Actions associated with a nursing care plan include monitoring the patient for signs of change or improvement, directly caring for the patient or conducting important medical tasks such as medication administration, educating and guiding the patient about further health management, and referring or contacting the patient for a follow-up.
